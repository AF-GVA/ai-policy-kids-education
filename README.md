# AI Policy Resources: Children, Education & Families

A curated list of resources for researchers, educators, policymakers, and parents navigating AI policy as it affects children and learning environments.

Maintained by [Alex Fayet](https://www.linkedin.com/in/alexfayet), founder of [SafeAI.School](https://safeai.school) — an independent, not-for-profit AI literacy platform for families and schools.

> **Scope:** This list focuses specifically on children (ages 0–18), families, schools, and educational contexts.

---

## International Frameworks & Guidance

### Children-Specific
- [UNICEF Policy Guidance on AI for Children 3.0 (2025)](https://www.unicef.org/globalinsight/reports/policy-guidance-ai-children) — 10 requirements and 48 recommendations. The primary international standard for child-centred AI governance.
- [UNICEF Policy Guidance on AI for Children 2.0 (2021)](https://www.unicef.org/globalinsight/reports/policy-guidance-ai-children-20)
- [UNICRI AI Literacy for Parents (2025)](https://unicri.it) — Practical guide for families on understanding AI risks and opportunities.
- [UNICEF — Children's Rights and Business Principles (AI application)](https://www.unicef.org/csr/css/AChildsRighttoaHealthyEnvironment_FINAL.pdf)

### Education-Specific
- [UNESCO Recommendation on the Ethics of AI (2021)](https://www.unesco.org/en/artificial-intelligence/recommendation-ethics)
- [UNESCO Guidance for Generative AI in Education and Research (2023)](https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/guidance-generative-ai-education-and-research)
- [UNESCO AI Competency Frameworks for Students and Teachers (2024)](https://www.unesco.org/en/digital-education/ai-future-learning)
- [OECD Digital Education Outlook 2026](https://www.oecd.org/education/oecd-digital-education-outlook-2023-c74f03de-en.htm) — Evidence base for AI use in learning systems.
- [OECD AI and Education: Guidance for Policy-makers (2021)](https://www.oecd.org/education/ai-and-education-9789264367197-en.htm)
- [European Commission — AI in Education: Ethical Guidelines for Teachers (2022)](https://www.eun.org/publications/detail?articleId=ai-ethical-guidelines-for-teachers)
- [Council of Europe — Digital Citizenship Education](https://www.coe.int/en/web/digital-citizenship-education)

### Regulatory (with direct implications for children & schools)
- [EU AI Act — Official Text (2024)](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32024R1689) — Article 50 (transparency obligations) and prohibited uses are directly relevant to children.
- [EU AI Act — High-Level Summary](https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/the-act/) — Accessible entry point.
- [GDPR & Children: Age of Digital Consent by Country](https://edpb.europa.eu/our-work-tools/our-documents/guidelines/guidelines-52020-consent-under-regulation-2016679_en) — Age 13 (platform default), 15 (France), 16 (Switzerland, Germany).
- [EU Digital Services Act — Child Safety Provisions](https://commission.europa.eu/strategy-and-policy/priorities-2019-2024/europe-fit-digital-age/digital-services-act_en)
- [UN Convention on the Rights of the Child — Digital Environment (General Comment No. 25)](https://www.ohchr.org/en/documents/general-comments-and-recommendations/general-comment-no-25-2021-childrens-rights-relation)

## Contribute
This list is a living document. To suggest a resource, open an issue or submit a pull request.

**Criteria for inclusion:**
- Primary source preferred (institutional, governmental, peer-reviewed)
- Specific to children, education, or families - not general AI policy
- Accessible (no paywall, or paywall noted)
- Last updated within the past 3 years (or foundational/historic value noted)
